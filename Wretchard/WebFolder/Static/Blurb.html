Principles of Information Operations

These principles ought to guide web development strategy and applications should be designed in accordance with them:

  1. Modern activity, including the economy, has become increasingly virtualized. Even the projection of force has acquired a narrative component.
  2. In a multiplicity of ways information has become the cornerstone of the modern world.
  3. The resulting information explosion had two immediate effects. First, the modern voter and consumer were overloaded with messages. There were suddenly too many newspapers, DVDs, TV channels, satellite radio programs and websites to possibly absorb. Second, the flood of information to which the public was exposed contained a large noise component. Political lies, marketing spin, hyperbole, or flat out fabrication.
  4. Social networks emerged as trust associations to deal with this information overload. They filter who you can trust and what reputations one recognzes.
  5. Social networks are the new villages. Any politician, marketer or social entrepreneur must ‘break into’ the village to spread a message but grow the social network to the maximum extent.

To an increasing extent the reputation of a firm, a political movement or an individual is an online image. More people will know you through the network than will ever meet you face-to-face. The value of this online reputation is the collective sum of the satisfaction felt by your affinity group as captured by the network.

It is a mistake to think that social networks are only about marketing. They are much more fundamentally about measuring reputation, ie ‘value’ of ideas, products and people. They are about measuring the truth through the eyes of those who have collectively considered it.
